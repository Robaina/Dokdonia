Overview

This page presents some of the evidence used to determine which pathways are present in Dokdonia sp. MED134. For each pathway, we show
  • A diagram (the pathway evidence glyph) summarizing the presence of enzymes for each reaction in the pathway
  • The normalized pathway score computed for the pathway
  • The total number of reactions in the pathway
  • The number of pathway reactions which an enzyme is present in Dokdonia sp. MED134
  • The number of those reactions that appear in other pathways in Dokdonia sp. MED134 (and hence are not unique to the particular pathway)
  • The number of reactions designated in MetaCyc as key indicators for the presence of this pathway, for which enzymes are present in Dokdonia sp. MED134
  • Whether this organism is within the taxonomic groups (if any) designated in MetaCyc as the taxonomic range of this pathway

Key to Pathway Evidence Glyph Edge Colors

  • Green: Enzyme present. An enzyme for this reaction has been identified in this organism.
  • Black: Enzyme absent. An enzyme for this reaction has not been identified in this organism.
  • Orange: Unique reaction. This reaction is unique to this pathway in the MetaCyc database.
  • Blue: Enzyme present by hole filler. An enzyme for this reaction has been identified in this organism by the Pathway Hole Filler.
  • Purple: Orphan reaction. No sequenced enzyme from any domain of life is known for this reaction.
  • Red: Key reaction. The reaction has been designated a key reaction of this pathway
  • Gray: Spontaneous/other. Used for spontaneous reactions, and for lines that do not denote reactions (e.g., denoting repetition in polymerization pathways).
